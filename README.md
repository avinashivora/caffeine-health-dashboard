## Caffeine & Health Dashboard

This dashboard analyzes the relationship between caffeine consumption patterns and key health indicators across different demographic and lifestyle groups. The objective was not simply to visualize caffeine intake, but to examine how frequency, source, timing, and dependency behaviors correlate with outcomes such as sleep quality, stress levels, heart rate trends, productivity, and overall wellness perception.

The report was designed to answer three core questions:

1. Which population segments consume the highest levels of caffeine?
2. How does caffeine intake influence physical and behavioral health metrics?
3. At what point does caffeine consumption shift from performance enhancement to measurable health impact?

The analysis combines comparative KPI tracking, behavioral segmentation, and trend-based visual exploration to surface actionable patterns rather than isolated statistics. Users can interactively filter the dashboard by age group, occupation, sleep duration, exercise frequency, beverage type, and daily caffeine intake ranges to observe how health indicators change across cohorts.

Key Features
- Consumption Pattern Analysis
- Tracks average daily caffeine intake, preferred caffeine sources, peak consumption hours, and intake frequency distributions.
- Health Correlation Monitoring
-  Evaluates associations between caffeine levels and metrics such as sleep efficiency, anxiety/stress scores, resting heart rate, hydration habits, and self-reported fatigue.
- Behavioral Segmentation
- Identifies high-dependency user groups and contrasts them against moderate and low-consumption populations to reveal differences in lifestyle and wellness outcomes.
- Interactive Comparative Views
- Enables multi-dimensional filtering for comparing demographic groups, work routines, and health behaviors in a single analytical workflow.
- Executive KPI Summary Layer
- Provides high-level insights into average consumption trends, risk indicators, and dominant behavioral patterns for quick decision-making.
- Technical Scope

The dashboard was developed in Microsoft Power BI using:

- Power Query for data transformation and normalization
- DAX measures for calculated health and intake metrics
- Relational data modeling for cross-dimensional analysis
- Interactive drill-through and slicer-based navigation for exploratory analysis

The project emphasizes analytical storytelling and user-driven exploration, making the dashboard suitable for health analytics demonstrations, behavioral research visualization, and business intelligence portfolio presentation.
